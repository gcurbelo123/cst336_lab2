History of Computers

Overview 1800s 1900s 2000s

In the 1800s, not many things happened when it came to the actual technology itself. The contributions around this time more had to do with abstract ideas and different math processes -- which are a huge part of computers and CS.

In France of 1801, Joseph Marie Jacquard invented a loom that used wooden cards to weave fabric designs. Now, this didn't necessarily enable computers to be created. Early computers used similar punch cards.

In 1822, Charles Babbage pitched a project that was funded by the English government. The project was a steam-driven calculating machine, that could compute tables of nubmers -- which is very similar to a modern day computer.

Then in 1890, Herman Hollerith designed a punch card system to calculate the 1880 census. He would later establish a company that we all know as IBM.
